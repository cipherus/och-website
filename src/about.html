Rethinking a Hackathon

The hackathon is radically different from other hackathons, because it is part of an experimental tech exhibit hosted by a unique media museum.

We're talking about Open Codes - Living in Digital Worlds of the ZKM in Karlsruhe. Unlike what you may think, it is not boring. In fact, it's filled with all kinds of awesome toys and gadgets, from VR goggles to mind-blowing interactive digital art. The exhibit sheds light on the world of devs, designers and makers, making the tech community more accessible to the general public.

As we all know, tech becomes ever more important, and yet it still has such a tough barrier of entry. We want to help change that, and to do so we needed something new. We had to rethink our hackathon.

Our approach: we make sure everyone, techies as well as museum visitors and everyone else is welcome at the OpenCodes Hackathon. Anyone can come visit the exhibit to see that a bunch of people sitting in a dark room with their hoods up is very far from the reality. Neither are we evil hackers who will steal your bank account. Instead hackathons have one of the coolest atmospheres, and that's what visitors will discover. We want to fascinate non-tech people with tech, and inspire them to join our ranks as hackers. Join us and be a part in this unique experiment.


For Literally Everyone

We want to motivate as many people as possible to join the hacking community. We want to provide an event where young hackers can work on their own pet projects, while experienced hackers can compete for prizes in ambitious categories.

We want to unite participants from all kinds of backgrounds. We want designers, organizers and artists to join the community of hackers and makers. We want people to pour all their creativity into their project in an inspiring environment.

Who are we?

We're a German student-run nonprofit organisation focused all around coding and hacking. We try to bring a bit of silicon valley to the german computer-science and start-up community by disrupting the status quo. We do this by organising hackathons, coding boot camps and other similar events. Our team consists of students from a variety of backgrounds: Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, Economics, Business, and more.


And now a bit about talKIT

We do the doing, talKIT does the talking. It's an amazing tech conference where people from all kinds of backgrounds come together to discuss one big topic. This year it's AI & cybersecurity, so get pumped!

TalKIT takes a look at this topic from many different angles, not just tech. It's a great experience and tons of fun. However you should know that the entire event is in German.
